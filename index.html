WHY STAVANGER?

Historical center for petroleum

Stavanger has always been ruled by the sea. The cathedral which laid the basis for the original town in the 12th century faces towards the Vågen inner harbour. Maritime routes were the channels for trade and external influences. In the 19th century, the town blossomed as a centre for the herring fishery and sailing ships.

The city stood ready to take on the role of Norway’s “oil capital” after the famed 1969 discovery. The first crude began to be produced in 1971.

My favorite places in Stavanger

Stavanger Art Museum

In scenic surroundings by Mosvannet in Stavanger lies the Stavanger Art Museum. The museum manages a collection of 3500 artworks, featuring both Norwegian and international, historical and contemporary art.

Address:

Henrik Ibsens gate 55 4021 Stavanger

What I like about it

The art museum is the perfect all-round day activity. A walk around the lake, a look through the current exhibitions and a coffee and cake from the museum cafe.

What I like about it

This cafe has it all. It's a great atmosphere to meet with friends or sit at the bar and chat to the employees on sights to see around the city. Whether you want a pick-me-up coffee and a book, or a beer with friends in the colorful backgarden, you can be sure you'll find a good time at Bøker.

Vålandstårnet

Located on Vålandshaugen, the tower, built in 1895, was originally constructed as a viewing tower and stands 84 meters above sea level. The café offers homemade pastries, cakes, and coffee.

Address:

Henrik Ibsens gate 55 4021 Stavanger

What I like about it

Vålandstårnet not only provides a great viewing point from which you can see Stavanger from above, but it is also surrounded by the lucious forest that allows for refreshing nature walks and picnics.
